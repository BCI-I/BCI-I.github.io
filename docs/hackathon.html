What

We are excited to announce MIT's first Brain Computer Interface (BCI) hackathon. Join us for a 48-hour event where teams of 3-5 MIT members will compete to devise the best algorithm to control a robot using only their brain.


The hackathon will be hosted at MIT in February 2024. It will be free and open to all MIT members. Winning teams will be awarded a cash prize. You can find more details below.

Why i

Brain Computer Interfaces (BCIs) enable direct control of computers and other devices using only brain activity. They are a rapidly growing field of research with applications in medicine, entertainment, and more and promise to revolutionize the way we interact with technology improve the lives of people suffering from neurological disorders & injuries.


Despite some awesome recent advances, a lot of work remains to be done before BCIs can be used in real-world applications. We want you to help advance the field of BCI research and development. This hackathon aims to bring together students from different backgrounds to learn about BCIs and kindle their interest in this field.
For the more entrepenurial participants winning the hackathon is also a great way to secure seed funding for your BCI venture.

The X.on EEG headset

Teams will use the X.on wireless EEG headset by Brain Products to record brain activity.

The X.on EEG headset

X.on is a new EEG headsets caractterized by its high signal quality, ease of use, and comfort.
It has 7 EEG channels, a ground, an integrated amplifier and the ability to wirelessly stream data through a Bluetooth connection. You can find additional information on the X.on headset and how to use it for the hackathon here.
